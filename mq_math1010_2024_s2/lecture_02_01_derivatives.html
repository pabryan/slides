1. The derivative

The derivative

The derivative

The derivative of \(f\) at \(x\) equals \[ \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \] provided the limit exists.

Derivative Notation

The derivative may be written as any of

  • \(\frac{df}{dx}\)
  • \(\frac{d}{dx} f\)
  • \(f'\)
  • \(\dot{f}\)

Example

Show that \[ \frac{d}{dx} x = 1 \]

Example

Show that \[ \frac{d}{dx} x^2 = 2x \]

2. Secant Line

Secant Line

Secant Line

The secant line for \(f(x)\) between \(x_1, x_2\) is the straight line though the points in the plane, \((x_1, f(x_1))\) and \((x_2, f(x_2))\).

Differences

\[ \Delta x = x_2 - x_1. \]

\[ \Delta f = f(x_2) - f(x_1). \]

Difference Quotient

The quantity \(\tfrac{\Delta f}{\Delta x}\) is called the difference quotient.

Secant Line Slope

The slope of the secant line is \[ \frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x} = \frac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{x_2 - x_1}. \]

Example

Let \(f(x) = x^2\) and let \(x_1 = 2\), \(x_2 = 3\). The secant line has equation \[ y = 5x - 6 \]

3. Tangent Line

Tangent Line

The tangent line at \(x\) is the line with slope \(f'(x)\) and passing through the point \((x, f(x))\) in the plane.

Tangent Line Slope

\[ f'(x_1) = \lim_{x_2 \to x_1} \frac{f(x_2) - f(x_1)}{x_2 - x_1} = \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x}. \]

Tangent Line and Secant Line

The tangent line at \(x\) is the limit of the secant lines as \(x_2 \to x_1\).

Example

Let \(f(x) = x^2\) and let \(x_1 = 2\).

Secant line has slope \[ 4 + \Delta x. \] Tangent line has slope \(4\)

4. Differentiability Implies Continuity

Differentiability Implies Continuity

Differentiability Implies Continuity

If \(f\) is differentiable at \(x_0\), then \(f\) is also continuous at \(x_0\).

\[ f(x) - f(x_0) = \frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x} \Delta x \to 0. \]
