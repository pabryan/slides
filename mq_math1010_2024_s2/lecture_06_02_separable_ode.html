Solving ODE's

General Solution

The General Solution includes arbitrary constants and we use the initial conditions to determine the values of these constants.

The arbitrary constants are constants of integration \[ F(x) = \int f(x) dx + C \]

2. Solving ODE's using FTC

Solving ODE's using FTC

FTC

\begin{align*} y'(t) &= f(t) \\ \Rightarrow y(t) &= y(t_0) + \int_{t_0}^t f(u) du \end{align*}

Example

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} y'(t) &= t^2 \\ y(1) &= 3 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Free Fall

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} z''(t) &= -g \\ z(0) &= z_0 \\ z'(0) &= v_0 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

3. Separable ODE's

Separable ODE's

Separable ODE's

A separable ODE is an ODE of the form \[ f(y) y' = g(t) \]

Exponential Growth/Decay

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} y' &= 3 y \\ y(0) &= 4 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Example

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} y' &= (x^2 - 4)(3y + 2) \\ y(0) &= -2 \end{cases} \end{equation*}
