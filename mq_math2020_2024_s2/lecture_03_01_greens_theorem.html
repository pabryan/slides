2. Green's Theorem

Green's Theorem

Green's Theorem

Let \(D \subseteq \RR^2\) be an open set with boundary \(C\). Then \[ \oint_C P dx + Q dy = \iint_{D} \partial_x Q - \partial_y P dA \]

  • \(C\) is oriented counter-clockwise so \(D\) is on the left when travelling around \(C\).

Example

\[ \oint_{C} 2y dx + 5x dy = \iint_D 5 - 2 dA \]

Area of an Ellipse

\[ \text{Area}(E) = \iint_E dA = \frac{1}{2} \int_C xdy - ydx \]

\[ \vecna{c}(t) = (a \cos t, b \sin t) \quad 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi \]

Example

Evaluate \[ \iint_{D} x^2 + y^2 dA \] where \(D\) is the region inside the disc, \(D_2\) of radius \(2\) centred on the origin and outside the disc, \(D_1\) of radius \(1\) centred on the origin.

3. Proof on a Rectangle

Proof on a Rectangle

Proof on a Rectangle

Let \(R\) be a rectangle with horizontal sides \(H_1, H_2\) and vertical sides \(V_1, V_2\)

\begin{align*} \oint_C \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds &= \int_{H_1} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds - \int_{H_2} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds \\ &= \int_{V_1} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds - \int_{V_2} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds \\ \end{align*}

Proof on a Rectangle

  • On \(H_1, H_2\) : \(\vec{T} = \vec{e_1}\) and \(\vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} = P\)
  • On \(V_1, V_2\) : \(\vec{T} = \vec{e_2}\) and \(\vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} = Q\)

Proof on a Rectangle

\begin{align*} -\int_a^b P(x, d) - P(x, c) dx &= -\int_a^b \int_c^d \partial_y P(x, y) dy dx \\ \int_c^d Q(b, y) - P(a, y) dy &= \int_c^d \int_a^b \partial_x Q(x, y) dx dy \end{align*}
