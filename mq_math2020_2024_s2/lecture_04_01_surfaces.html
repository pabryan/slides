2. Regular Parametrised Surfaces

Regular Parametrised Surfaces

Regular Parametrision

A regular parametrisation for a surface is a \(C^1\) map, \[ \vec{r}(u, v) = \big(x(u, v), y(u, v), z(u, v)\big) \] such that the differential \(d\vec{r}\) is injective.

Regular Parametrision

  • Domain: \((u, v) \in U\) an open set of \(\RR^2\)
  • Injectivity: columns \(\partial_u \vec{r}, \partial_v \vec{r}\) linearly independent

Regular Parametrised Surfaces

A regular (parametrised) surface \(S\) is the image of a regular parametrisation.

3. Examples

Examples

Paraboloid

\[ \vec{r}(u, v) = (u, v, u^2 + v^2) \]

Sphere

\[ \vec{r}(\theta, \varphi) = (\sin\varphi \cos\theta, \sin\varphi \sin\theta, \cos\varphi) \] \[ 0 < \theta < 2\pi, \quad 0 < \varphi < \pi \]

Torus

\begin{align*} x(\theta, \varphi) &= (R_{\text{out}} + R_{\text{in}} \cos \theta)\cos\varphi \\ y(\theta, \varphi) &= (R_{\text{out}} + R_{\text{in}} \cos \theta)\sin\varphi \\ z(\theta, \varphi) &= R_{\text{in}} \sin \theta \end{align*}

Surfaces of Revolution

Let \(f : (a, b) \to \RR\) be a positive function.

Surface of revolution of \(f\) around the \(z\) axis: \[ \vec{r} (t, \theta) = (f(t) \cos\theta, f(t) \sin\theta, t) \]

Surfaces of Revolution: Examples

  • Sphere: \(f = \sqrt{1 - t^2}\)
  • Cylinder: \(f = 1\)
  • Paraboloid \(f = t^2\)

Hyperboloids

  • Upper sheet of two sheeted hyperboloid: \(f = \sqrt{1 + t^2}\)
    • \(x^2 + y^2 - z^2 = -1\)
    • \(\vec{r}(\theta, \varphi) = (\cosh \varphi \cos\theta, \cosh \varphi \sin\theta, \sinh \varphi)\) (hyperbolic polar coords)
  • One sheeted hyperboloid: \(f = \sqrt{t^2 - 1}\)
