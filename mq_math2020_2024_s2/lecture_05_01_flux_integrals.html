2. Normal Vector

Normal Vector

Normal Vector

The positively oriented unit normal in a parametrisation is \[ \vec{N} = \frac{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v}{\abs{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v}} \]

Normal Vector

\(\vec{N}\) is perpendicular to \(S\)

\(\vec{N} \cdot \vec{e}_u = \vec{N} \cdot \vec{e}_v = 0\)

Orientation

Positive orientation is the right hand rule. Negative orientation is the left hand rule.

Orientation Examples

  • Positive: \(\big(\vec{e}_u, \vec{e}_v, \vec{N}\big)\), \(\big(\vec{e}_v, \vec{e}_u, -\vec{N}\big)\)
  • Negative: \(\big(\vec{e}_u, \vec{e}_v, -\vec{N}\big)\), \(\big(\vec{e}_v, \vec{e}_u, \vec{N}\big)\)
  • Sphere: \(\vec{N}(p) = p\)
  • \(xy\)-plane: \(\vec{N} = \vec{e}_3\)
  • Cylinder: \(\vec{N}(x, y, z) = (x, y, 0)\)
  • Graph \(z = f(x, y)\): \(\vec{N} = \frac{\vec{e}_3 - \nabla f}{\sqrt{1 + \abs{\nabla f}^2}}\)

3. Flux Integrals

Flux Integrals

Flux Integrals

The flux of a vector field \(\vec{F}\) across a surface \(S\) is

\begin{align*} \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA &= \iint_U \vec{F} \cdot \frac{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v}{\abs{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v}} \abs{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v} \, du dv \\ &= \iint_U \vec{F} \cdot \big(\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v\big) \, du dv \end{align*}

Flux Integrals

  • Flux integrals are independent of parametrisation up to orientation
  • Change of orientation \(\vec{N} \mapsto -\vec{N}\) changes the sign of the flux integral

\(S = \{-1 < x < 1, -1 < y < 1, z = 0\}\)

\(\vec{F} = (xz^2, e^y, x e^z)\)

\(S = \{x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1\}\)

\(\vec{F_1} = (x, y, z)\), \(\vec{F_2} = (-y, x, 0)\)

\(S = \{x^2 + y^2 = 1\}\)

\(\vec{F_1} = (x, y, z)\), \(\vec{F_2} = (-y, x, 0)\)
