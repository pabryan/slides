2. Gauss' Law

Gauss' Law

Gauss' Law

Let \(\vec{F}(p) = \frac{p}{r^3}\)

Then for any closed surface \(S\) enclosing the region \(\Omega\)

\begin{equation*} \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA = \begin{cases} 4\pi & 0 \in \Omega \\ 0 & 0 \notin \overline{\Omega} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
  • If \(0 \notin \overline{\Omega}\) then \(\vec{F}\) is defined on \(\Omega\)
  • \(\divg \vec{F} = 0\) on \(\Omega\)
  • Divergence theorem \[ \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA = \iiint_{\Omega} \divg \vec{F} dV = 0 \]
  • \(0 \in \overline{\Omega}\): \(\vec{F}\) defined on \(\Omega_{\epsilon} = \Omega \backslash \mathbb{B}_{\epsilon}(0)\)
  • \(\divg \vec{F} = 0\) on \(\Omega_{\epsilon}\)
  • Divergence theorem \[ 0 =\iiint_{\Omega_{\epsilon}} \divg \vec{F} dV = \iint_{\partial \Omega_{\epsilon}} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA \]
  • \[ 0 =\iiint_{\Omega_{\epsilon}} \divg \vec{F} dV = \iint_{\partial \Omega_{\epsilon}} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA \]
  • \(\partial \Omega_{\epsilon} = S - \mathbb{S}_{\epsilon}\)
  • \[ \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA = \iint_{\mathbb{S}_{\epsilon}} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA \]
  • \(\vec{N}(p) = \frac{p}{\epsilon}\)

  • Then

    \begin{align*} \iint_{\mathbb{S}_{\epsilon}} \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} dA &= \iint_{\mathbb{S}_{\epsilon}} \frac{p}{\epsilon^3} \cdot \frac{p}{\epsilon} dA \\ &= \iint_{\mathbb{S}_{\epsilon}} \frac{1}{\epsilon^2} dA \\ &= 4\pi \end{align*}

3. Electric Field

Electric Field

Electric Field

The flux of the electric field through a surface \(S\) is proportional to the enclosed charge.

  • point charge: \(\vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0} \frac{p}{r^3}\)
  • multiple point charges: superposition (linearity)

4. Maxwell's Equations

Maxwell's Equations

Maxwell's Equations

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \divg \vec{E} &= \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0} \\ \curl \vec{E} &= - \partial_t \vec{B} \\ \divg \vec{B} &= 0 \\ \curl \vec{B} &= \mu_0\left(\vec{J} + \epsilon_0 \partial_t \vec{E}\right) \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Faraday Cage

A perfectly conducting, closed surface \(S\) shields any external electrostatic field.

\begin{align*} \vec{E} &= \nabla \varphi \\ \varphi|_S &\equiv \text{ constant} \\ \Delta \varphi &:= \divg \nabla \varphi = 0 \end{align*}
