2. Divergence

Divergence

Divergence

The divergence of a vector field \(\vec{F}\) is the scalar field

\begin{align*} \divg \vec{F} &= \nabla \cdot \vec{F} \\ &= \partial_{x_1} F_1 + \dots + \partial_{x_n} F_n \end{align*}

Examples

\begin{align*} \divg \left(x\vec{e}_1 + y \vec{e}_2\right) &= 2 \\ \divg \left(x\vec{e}_1 - y \vec{e}_2\right) &= 0 \\ \divg \left(\vec{e}_1 + \vec{e}_2\right) &= 0 \\ \end{align*}

3. Curl

Curl

Curl

The curl of \(F = (P, Q, R)\) is the vector field

\begin{align*} \curl F &= \nabla \times F \\ &= \left(\partial_y R - \partial_z Q\right) \vec{e}_1 \\ &\quad - \left(\partial_x R - \partial_z P\right) \vec{e}_2 \\ &\quad + \left(\partial_x Q - \partial_y P\right) \vec{e}_3 \end{align*}

Curl

\[ F = (x^2z, e^y + xz, xyz) \]

\[ \curl F = (xz - x)\vec{e}_1 + (x^2 - yx) \vec{e}_2 + z \vec{e}_3 \]

4. Green's Theorem

Green's Theorem

2d Curl

For \(\vec{F} = (P, Q)\), the \(2\)-d curl is the scalar field \[ \curl_2 \vec{F} = \partial_x Q - \partial_y P \]

  • \(\curl_2 (P, Q) = \curl (P, Q, 0) \cdot \vec{e}_3\)

Green's Theorem: Curl Form

\[ \int_C \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds = \iint_D \curl_2 \vec{F} dA \]

Green's Theorem: Div Form

\[ \int_C \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} ds = \iint_D \divg \vec{F} dA \]

  • \(C\) is oriented counter-clockwise and \(\vec{N}\) is the unit outer normal.

Example

\[ \vec{F} = (x^3, y^3), \quad C = (\cos t, \sin t) \quad 0 \leq t \leq 2\pi \]
