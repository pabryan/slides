2. Area of Surfaces

Area of Surfaces

Coordinate Tangent Vectors

Let \(\vec{r}\) be a regular parametrisation The coordinate tangent vectors are:

\begin{align*} \vec{e}_u &= \partial_u \vec{r} \\ \vec{e}_v &= \partial_v \vec{r} \end{align*}

Element of Area

The element of area for a regular surface is \[ dA = \abs{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v} \, dudv \]

Area Integrals

The area of a surface is \[ \text{Area} (S) = \iint_S dA = \iint_U \abs{\vec{e}_v \times \vec{e}_v} \, du dv \]

Area of the Sphere

\begin{align*} \vec{r}(\theta, \varphi) &= (\sin\varphi \cos\theta, \sin\varphi \sin\theta, \cos\varphi) \\ \vec{e}_{\theta} &= \big(-\sin\varphi \sin\theta, \sin\varphi \cos\theta, 0 \big) \\ \vec{e}_{\varphi} &= \big(\cos\varphi \cos\theta, \cos\varphi \sin\theta, -\sin\varphi) \\ \vec{e}_{\theta} \times \vec{e}_{\varphi} &= -\sin\varphi \vec{r} \end{align*}

Area of the Sphere

\begin{align*} \vec{e}_{\theta} \times \vec{e}_{\varphi} &= -\sin\varphi \vec{r} \\ dA &= \abs{\vec{e}_{\theta} \times \vec{e}_{\varphi}} \,d\varphi d\theta = \sin\varphi \, d\varphi d\theta \\ \text{Area} &= \iint dA = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\pi} \sin\varphi \, d\varphi d\theta = 4\pi \end{align*}

3. Scalar Integrals

Scalar Integrals

Scalar Surface Integrals

The integral of \(f\) over \(S\) is defined by \[ \iint_S f \> dA = \iint_D f(\vec{r}(u, v)) \, \abs{\vec{e}_u \times \vec{e}_v} \, du dv \]

Let \(f(x, y, z) = x^2\) and let \(S\) be the unit sphere

\begin{align*} f \circ \vec{r}(\theta, \varphi) &= f (\cos\theta\sin\varphi, \sin\theta\sin\varphi, \cos\varphi) \\ &= \cos^2\theta \sin^2\varphi \\ dA &= \sin\varphi d\varphi d\theta\ \end{align*}

\[ \iint_{S} x^2 \, dA = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\pi} \cos^2\theta \sin^3\varphi \, d\varphi d\theta = \frac{4\pi}{3} \]

Let \(f(x, y, z) = x^2\) and let \(S\) be the cylinder \[ \{(x, y, z) : x^2 + y^2 = 1, \, -1 < z < 1\} \]

\[ \vec{r} (t, \theta) = (\cos \theta, \sin \theta, t) \] \[ \iint_S x^2 dA = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_{-1}^{1} \cos^2 \theta \, dt d\theta = 2\pi \]
