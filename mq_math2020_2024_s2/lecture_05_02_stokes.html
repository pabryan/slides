2. Stokes' Theorem

Stokes' Theorem

Curl form of Green's Theorem

Let \(U \subseteq \RR^2\) have regular boundary curve \(C\). For any vector field on \(\RR^3\) of the form \(\vec{F} = (P, Q, 0)\) we have \[ \iint_U \curl \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} = \int_C \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds \] where \(\vec{N} = \vec{e}_3\).

Stokes' Theorem

Let \(S \subseteq \RR^3\) be a regular surface with unit normal \(\vec{N}\) and regular boundary curve \(\partial S = C\).

For any vector field \(\vec{F}\) on \(\RR^3\) we have \[ \iint_S \curl \vec{F} \cdot \vec{N} = \int_C \vec{F} \cdot \vec{T} ds \]

  • \(S = \{x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1, z \geq 0\}\)
  • \(C = \{x^2 + y^2 =1 , z = 0\}\)
  • \(\vec{F} = (-y, 0, x)\)

3. Calculating Flux Integrals

Calculating Flux Integrals

\(F = (x, 2xy, x + y)\)

  • hemisphere \(\{x^2 + y^2 + (z-1)^2 = 1, z \geq 1\}\)
  • capped cylinder \(C \cup D\):
    • \(C = \{x^2 + y^2 = 1, 1 \leq z \leq 2\}\)
    • \(D = \{x^2 + y^2 \leq 1, z = 2\}\)
  • boundary \(S\) the unit circle in the \(z=1\) plane

Surface Independence

The flux of a curl is independent of the surface. It depends only on the boundary curve.
